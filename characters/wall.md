## First Observation

Wall is another dwarf. This one is more on brand: compact, direct, armored in intent before the armor is even considered. Where MrBeast makes himself quiet around the edges, Wall presents as an answer to a question the enemy has not yet asked.

He carries sword and shield and intends to be between his party and everything else. Not beside. Not generally near. Between. This is the whole doctrine, and unlike many doctrines, it improves by not being complicated.

## The Brunt

Wall intends to take the brunt of damage. This is not phrased as aspiration. It is logistics. If danger has a path, he will occupy it. If the party has softer members, and it does, he will make himself the surface against which the world first spends its force.

The original description offered by the table was more anatomically informal. The chronicle, seeking a phrasing suitable for preservation, records the substance instead: Wall appears to be the opposite of a coward.
